Live-Streaming Assistant

Streaming Assistant is an AI-assisted live streaming companion system designed to help streamers manage real-time audience interaction, emotional pressure, and cultural communication risks during broadcasts. The product is an AI-assisted live-streaming companion system that combines a camera w/ display stand, a pressure-sensitive footpad, microphone input, LED reminders, and AI analysis to help streamers manage audience interaction without breaking their performance flow. The concept is based on three real-time input channels: audience comment analysis, streamer monitoring, and foot-controlled interaction. The project combines Human-Computer Interaction, industrial design and AI-based feedback systems to create a low-intervention “backstage assistant” that supports streamers without interrupting their natural performance.

The system includes a facial recognition camera, OLED feedback display, microphone, LED reminder strip, and a pressure-sensitive footpad interface. While the camera and microphone monitor the streamer’s facial expression and speech, the AI analyzes live comments, audience sentiment, trending keywords, and potential cultural risks. Instead of forcing the streamer to constantly read fast-moving chat messages, the system summarizes key information into subtle visual cues and real-time suggestions. A key interaction feature is the foot-controlled pad, which allows streamers to trigger quick actions without breaking eye contact with the camera. Through subtle foot movements, the streamer can activate functions such as topic generation, sentiment analysis, quick polls, mini-games, warm-up interactions, or blocking disruptive viewers. This makes the system especially useful during cold moments, audience drop-offs, hostile comments, or moments when the streamer needs fast support while staying visually present on camera. The design responds to several pain points found through background research and interviews: streamers often struggle with visual overload, delayed audience feedback, emotional vulnerability, interaction stagnation, and the pressure of managing performance and chat at the same time. The final concept positions AI not as a replacement for the streamer, but as a quiet support layer that improves control, awareness, and confidence while preserving authenticity and personal expression.
